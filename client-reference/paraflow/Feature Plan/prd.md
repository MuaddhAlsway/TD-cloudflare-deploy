## 1) Background

TD Logistics needs a professional website that serves both B2C end-customers seeking fast shipment tracking and B2B e-commerce clients looking for reliable logistics partnerships. Current market solutions force businesses to choose between consumer-friendly interfaces and professional B2B presentations, creating missed opportunities for lead generation and customer satisfaction. This addresses the Charter's core problems of logistics uncertainty and service inconsistencies by providing transparent, accessible communication channels that build trust with both audience segments simultaneously. This is the right time to launch because TD Logistics has established operational excellence and needs digital presence to match their service quality and capture market growth in Saudi Arabia's expanding e-commerce sector.

## 2) Objectives & Desired Outcomes

- **Seamless Dual Experience**: Both Aisha (B2C) and Khalid (B2B) find their specific needs immediately addressed without compromising the other's experience through intelligent information architecture and prominent feature placement.
- **Trust and Credibility Building**: Potential B2B clients gain confidence in TD Logistics' capabilities through authentic visual evidence, client testimonials, and professional service presentation, while end-customers feel secure about their shipment handling.
- **Operational Efficiency**: Customer service load decreases as users find answers and take actions independently through self-service tracking, comprehensive service information, and accessible support channels.
- **Lead Generation Success**: Qualified B2B prospects submit detailed quote requests with sufficient information for sales team follow-up, while existing clients easily access account management features.
- **Non-goals / Boundaries**: This iteration focuses on information presentation and lead capture rather than full client portal functionality or real-time logistics system integration.

## 3) Users & Stories

- **Primary Persona:** Aisha Al-Mahmoud (B2C End-Customer)
  - Story A: As Aisha, I want to quickly enter my tracking number on the homepage and see clear delivery status, so I can plan my schedule and feel confident about my purchase.
  - Story B: As Aisha, I want to instantly connect with customer support through WhatsApp or chat when I have delivery concerns, so I can resolve issues without phone wait times.
  - Story C: As Aisha, I want to access tracking from any page through a persistent button, so I don't have to navigate back to the homepage when I'm browsing TD Logistics services.

- **Secondary Persona:** Khalid bin Hassan (B2B E-commerce Client)
  - Story D: As Khalid, I want to see detailed service information about COD and reverse logistics with client success examples, so I can evaluate if TD Logistics meets my business requirements.
  - Story E: As Khalid, I want to submit a comprehensive quote request with my specific business needs and volume estimates, so I can receive accurate pricing and partnership proposals.
  - Story F: As Khalid, I want to see authentic client logos and testimonials on the homepage, so I can validate TD Logistics' credibility before investing time in partnership discussions.

## 4) Key Feature

- **Prominent Tracking Module**: Homepage features a large, clear shipment tracking interface similar to sal.sa design that provides immediate access to delivery status without registration or account creation, with real-time status updates and delivery estimates.
- **Dual-Purpose Homepage Design**: Strategic layout that serves both audiences simultaneously with tracking prominence for B2C users and trust signals, service highlights, and quote CTAs for B2B prospects, without cluttering or compromising either experience.
- **Comprehensive Service Portfolio Pages**: Detailed pages for Last-Mile Delivery, E-commerce Solutions (COD/Returns), Customized Delivery, and Technology services that provide B2B prospects with thorough capability understanding and differentiation details.
- **Professional Quote Request System**: Multi-step form capturing business requirements, service needs, volume estimates, and contact preferences that enables sales team to provide accurate proposals and partnership recommendations.
- **Persistent Support Access**: Floating WhatsApp/chat icon available site-wide for instant customer support, plus dedicated sales inquiry channels and client login access in main navigation for business users.
- **Authentic Trust Building**: Homepage client logo showcase, realistic fleet/warehouse imagery, and testimonial integration that demonstrates proven capability and builds credibility with potential B2B partners.

## 5) Key Flow

- **Example:** B2C Shipment Tracking
  - **Trigger:** Aisha receives SMS with tracking number and wants to check delivery status
  - **Path:** Enters tracking number in prominent homepage module, views real-time status and estimated delivery time, optionally contacts support via floating chat icon if issues arise
  - **Result:** Complete visibility into shipment status with immediate support access if needed

- **Example:** B2B Partnership Evaluation
  - **Trigger:** Khalid searches for logistics partners and discovers TD Logistics website | **Path:** Reviews services pages, examines client testimonials and social proof, submits detailed quote request through Sales & Partnerships page | **Result:** Comprehensive understanding of TD capabilities with sales consultation scheduled

- **Example:** Existing Client Access
  - **Trigger:** Current client needs to access account dashboard or submit service requests | **Path:** Clicks Client Login in main navigation, authenticates, and accesses dedicated client portal features | **Result:** Direct access to account management and ongoing service tools

## 6) Competitive Analysis

- **Landscape (who is solving this problem):** SAL (Saudi Post), Aramex, DHL Express, SMSA Express serve logistics needs with consumer or B2B focus, manual alternatives include direct delivery or multiple vendor relationships, and generic logistics companies provide basic tracking without specialization.

- **Value Thesis (each player's proposition):** SAL offers government-backed reliability but limited B2B specialization, Aramex provides international reach with complex pricing, DHL focuses on premium express services for large businesses, SMSA offers local market knowledge with traditional service models, while manual solutions provide control but require significant operational overhead.

- **Strengths / Weaknesses (experience pros/cons):** SAL has simple tracking interface but lacks B2B partnership focus, international carriers offer comprehensive services but with complexity that overwhelms smaller businesses, local competitors provide market knowledge but often lack professional digital presence and modern customer experience standards.

- **Our Differentiators (our unique points):** TD Logistics combines reliability and professional excellence with clarity and simplicity to serve both audiences equally well through authentic leadership demonstration, offering high-touch service quality with accessible technology interfaces, specializing in Saudi market needs without the complexity or generic approach of larger competitors.

- **Switching Costs & Risks (migration costs and risks):** B2B clients face integration effort and relationship rebuilding when changing logistics partners, while end-customers may resist learning new tracking systems, requiring TD to demonstrate superior value and smooth onboarding processes.
